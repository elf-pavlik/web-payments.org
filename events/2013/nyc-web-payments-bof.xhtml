NYC Web Payments BoF

 

Topic: Web Payments and Enabling Price Stability

  • Who: Web Payments innovators, Bitcoiners, Ripplers, and Economists
  • When: 6:30 pm on Sunday, September 22nd 2013
  • Where: New York City (NYU or a quiet restaurant around NYU)
 

Scenario #1: Jane wants to accept Bitcoins as advance payment in February for the vegetables that she will grow on her farm as part of a seasonal community-supported agriculture program. She is concerned that the value of a Bitcoin will change so drastically that by the time she is delivering vegetables, the portion of her advance funding for harvest operations will have halved in value and she will be unable to afford to run her farm during delivery. A new economic tool is needed to ensure the price stability of a Bitcoin so that she can accept Bitcoins for long-term contracts without fear of their devaluation over time.

Scenario #2: If a company in Canada signed a six-month contract in March 2013 with a software developer in Brazil in which payment was denominated in either Brazilian Reals or Canadian Dollars, then by the end of the contract in August the buyer and seller would have experienced more than a 10% difference in purchasing power compared with the beginning of the contract. Currency hedging techniques typically add complexity and involve service fees, so they may or may not be advisable.

How do we create an open payment standard for the Web that permits the stable interpretation of prices for tangible goods and services in various currencies? Can the Web Payments Community Group come up with ways to offer price stability for contracts that involve deferred payments, even when currency exchange rates fluctuate constantly in the speculative forex market? Can we invent ways to express stable value with decentralized virtual currencies such as Bitcoin (BTC) and Ripples (XRP) in spite of general currency system turbulence? Join us to learn about, discuss, and explore new ways of approaching the challenges and opportunities of open funding and open payment protocols on the Web.
