Open Marketplaces Incubator Group Charter

The mission of the Open Marketplaces Incubator Group, part of the Incubator Activity, is to determine a set of technologies that should be utilized or created for the purpose of enabling the distributed listing, discovery and exchange of digital goods and services on the Web.

Scope

  • Developing ideas around Secure Web ID for digital signatures and online transactions.
  • Developing ideas around PaySwarm and digital contracts, licenses, and collaborative content distribution.
  • Development of use cases and requirements for open marketplaces.
  • Catalog pre-existing standards that can be used for open marketplaces.
  • Identify missing technologies and/or specifications requiring further development.
  • Propose a work plan for a Working Group that would develop the technologies required for open marketplaces.

Success Criteria

The success of the Open Marketplaces XG depends on the generation of a set of use cases, a technology review document, and a proposed working group charter to enable open marketplaces on the Web.

Out of Scope

The following items are out-of-scope for the open marketplaces XG:

  • Markup languages and ontologies for media licensing expression.
  • Technology that ignores copyright or other intellectual property concerns.
  • Indexing or search mechanisms for open marketplaces.
  • In-browser Javascript APIs for open marketplaces.
  • New peer-to-peer communication protocols that do not sit on top of the Web.

Deliverables

  • A Use Cases document for open marketplaces.
  • A Technology Review document outlining potential and needed technologies for open marketplaces.
  • A Working Group charter document for enabling open marketplaces.

Participation

Participants are expected to subscribe to the public mailing list and take part in the discussion. There will be bi-monthly teleconferences to discuss pressing issues or to interact with invited speakers.

Communication

This group primarily conducts its work on the public mailing list public-xg-open-marketplaces@w3.org (archive) . There is no Member-only group list.

Information about the group (deliverables, participants, teleconferences, etc.) is available from the Open Marketplaces Incubator Group home page.

Decision Policy

As explained in the Process Document (section 3.3), this group will seek to make decisions when there is consensus. When the Chair puts a question and observes dissent, after due consideration of different opinions, the Chair should record a decision (possibly after a formal vote) and any objections, and move on.

  • When deciding a substantive technical issue, the Chair may put a question before the group. The Chair must only do so during a group meeting, and at least two-thirds of participants in Good Standing must be in attendance. When the Chair conducts a formal vote to reach a decision on a substantive technical issue, eligible voters may vote on a proposal one of three ways: for a proposal, against a proposal, or abstain. For the proposal to pass there must be more votes for the proposal than against. In case of a tie, the Chair will decide the outcome of the proposal.
  • This charter is written in accordance with Section 3.4, Votes of the W3C Process Document and includes no voting procedures beyond what the Process Document requires.

Patent Policy

This Incubator Group provides an opportunity to share perspectives on the topic addressed by this charter. W3C reminds Incubator Group participants of their obligation to comply with patent disclosure obligations as set out in Section 6 of the W3C Patent Policy. While the Incubator Group does not produce Recommendation-track documents, when Incubator Group participants review Recommendation-track specifications from Working Groups, the patent disclosure obligations do apply.

Incubator Groups have as a goal to produce work that can be implemented on a Royalty Free basis, as defined in the W3C Patent Policy.

For more information about disclosure obligations for this group, please see the W3C Patent Policy Implementation.
