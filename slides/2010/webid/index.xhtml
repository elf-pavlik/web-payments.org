Consider how we use identity...

  • Phone - you can phone anybody across systems and social networks.
  • E-mail - you can e-mail anybody across systems and social networks.
  • Web - You can identify web pages using global identifiers.
  • However, your web identities are not interoperable - there is no global identity. You can't take your friends with you from Facebook, to LinkedIn, to Twitter. Your identity exists in a silo - a walled garden.
The most basic type of identity...

Username/Password

  • Purpose: By identifying a person, you can determine their rights to site resources.
  • Advantages: widely deployed, well understood
  • Disadvantages:
    • Usability: username/password memorization, username/password synchronization, passwords are not secure - "solutions" are password managers and Firefox Weave
    • Technical: no global identity, account maintenance, specifying friends and contact information repeatedly is tedious, no way of enabling Linked Data
Toward a global identity...

OpenID Video Demonstration

OpenID

  • Purpose: Global identity and authentication.
  • Advantages: one identity across all domains, protected name/value pair attribute transfer, partly distributed/partly RESTful
  • Disadvantages
    • Usability: have to type in your username as a URI
    • Technical: requires many connections (7-8 HTTPS connections), awkward/unRESTful attribute exchange protocol, no strong crypto support for digital signatures or encryption, difficult to change OpenID providers w/o starting from scratch
  • Implementers: Google, SixApart, Yahoo!, Flickr, MySpace, Facebook, Wordpress, Verisign, AOL, and many more...
Supporting architecture for OpenID...

WebFinger and .well-known/

  • Purpose (WebFinger): Discover a person's profile via their e-mail address.
  • Purpose (.well-known): Directory for well known files such as robots.txt, avatar images, host-meta (used by OpenID), etc.
  • Advantages: Simple to deploy, supporting technology for OpenID and WebID
  • Disadvantages: Doesn't solve the authentication problem, .well-known spec states that it is a hack - not good for the Web, WebFinger uses complicated XRD file format.
  • Implementers: Google and AOL
A simpler, more powerful identification protocol...

WebID Video Demonstration

WebID

  • Purpose: Web architecture-compliant identity and authentication protocol for the Social Web.
  • Description of how it works
  • Advantages
    • Usability: No password or URL to remember, no need to repeatedly enter friends and contacts, potential for browser-integration, very high security (TLS)
    • Technical: wide support in desktop browsers, RESTful authentication, peer-to-peer identity, simplified identification protocol, supports digital signatures (X509), supports strong encryption of messages (TLS), extensibility via Linked Data (RDF, RDFa), syntax agnostic (easy to integrate w/ Portable Contacts, etc.)
  • Disadvantages: Inability to log out, user-interfaces, uniform UI across all browsers, does not work on older cellphones - patchy support on newer ones

WebID Enables

WebID is an enabling technology for...

  • Web payments and micro-financing - PaySwarm
  • Decentralized social messaging - Identi.ca, Diaspora, etc.
  • eGovernment and verifiable online elections - Helios
  • Secrets in the cloud via Nigori
  • The Social Web
