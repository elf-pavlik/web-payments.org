Web Payments Intro

 

Getting Started with Web Payments.

 

The purpose of the Web Payments group at the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is to create systems that enable people and businesses on the Web to send each other money as easily as they exchange instant messages and email today. The systems strive to be easy to use, currency agnostic, secure, decentralized and implementable by anyone in a patent and royalty-free manner. The group is focusing on transforming the way we reward each other on the Web as well as how we organize financial resources to enhance our personal lives and pursue endeavors that improve upon the human condition.

It can be difficult to know where to start, so this page has been created to help bring you up to speed with the state of Web Payments. It contains links to introductory material related to a variety of the work that is currently happening in the Web Payments group.

 

An introduction to PaySwarm

 

PaySwarm is the worlds first universal payment standard for the Web, making single-click purchases and crowdfunding on the Web a reality, regardless of who your bank or financial institution is. It's like PayPal, only open, patent and royalty-free. The introduction to PaySwarm is split into 3 parts:

Learning about Bitcoin

 

Bitcoin is the worlds first decentralized digital currency. It is the most successful attempt so far at a completely decentralized financial network based on mathematics and the Internet.

An overview of Ripple

 

Ripple is a peer-to-peer payment system. It lets you easily, cheaply, and safely send money over the Internet to anyone, anywhere in the world. Anyone can create an account. Because Ripple is P2P software, no individual, corporation, or government controls it. The network operates via the combined effort of the Ripple software running all over the world.
